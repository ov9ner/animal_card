White-tailed deer

Their special stomachs allow them to eat some things humans cannot, such as mushrooms and poison ivy.

  • Scientific Name: Odocoileus virginianus
  • Length: Ranges from 95 to 220 cm
  • Average Lifespan: 4.5 years
  • Habitat: southern Canada and all of the mainland United States

The deer can be recognized by the characteristic white underside to its tail. It raises its tail when it is alarmed to warn the predator that it has been detected. It usually weighs about 45 kilograms
